Closing Comments

Degeneracies:
Based on the multiple cases tested, we can say with reasonable certainity that most of the degenerate cases such as horizontal lines or collinear vertices on the hull.
The horizontal segments are easily handled by the "titled sweep line" method wherein we prefer the lower x coordinate. For collinear vertices, including the angle = 0 case in the "left turn" group ensures that the vertex remains on the hull.
In the case of Graham's Scan, conversion to polar coordinates can cause floating point error, which though minor in terms of absolute magnitude, can shift points by enough that the hull changes. This was countered by taking a very small margin of 10^-6 during the comparision.
This margin should be decided based on the input and shouldn't be a generic fixed value.
Complexity:
The base algorithms are all O(nlogn) provided that the sorting and status have optimal complexities.
Robustness:
All functions return an integer to denote the success of the operation that had been carried out. If a method returns 0, then further steps are not executed and the user is given an appropriate error message.
